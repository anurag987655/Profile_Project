# Simplicial Complex

A simplicial complex is a fundamental object in algebraic topology and computational geometry. It generalises the idea of a graph by including higher-dimensional analogues of edges — triangles, tetrahedra, and beyond.

## Definition

An **abstract simplicial complex** $K$ over a finite set of vertices $V$ is a collection of non-empty subsets of $V$ such that:

1. If $\sigma \in K$ and $\tau \subseteq \sigma$ with $\tau \neq \emptyset$, then $\tau \in K$.
2. $\{v\} \in K$ for every $v \in V$.

A subset $\sigma \in K$ of size $k+1$ is called a **$k$-simplex**. A $0$-simplex is a vertex, a $1$-simplex is an edge, a $2$-simplex is a triangle, and a $3$-simplex is a tetrahedron.

## Geometric Realisation

Every abstract simplicial complex can be realised geometrically as a topological space. The geometric $k$-simplex is the convex hull of $k+1$ affinely independent points in $\mathbb{R}^n$:

$$\Delta^k = \left\{ \sum_{i=0}^{k} t_i e_i \;\middle|\; t_i \geq 0,\; \sum_{i=0}^{k} t_i = 1 \right\}$$

The geometric realisation $|K|$ is formed by gluing together geometric simplices along their faces according to the inclusion structure of $K$.

## Applications

- **Topological Data Analysis**: Persistent homology computes topological features of point cloud data by constructing a Vietoris–Rips or Čech complex at multiple scales.
- **Mesh Generation**: Finite element methods use simplicial complexes (triangulations) to discretise continuous domains.
- **Network Science**: Higher-order interactions beyond pairwise edges can be modelled using simplicial complexes.

---

### Key Takeaways

- Simplicial complexes extend graphs to higher dimensions.
- They are built from simplices closed under taking faces.
- Their geometry and homology reveal structure in data and shape.
